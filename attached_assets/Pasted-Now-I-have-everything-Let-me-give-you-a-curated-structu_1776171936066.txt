Tier 1: Study These Real Products (screenshot their UIs for your Figma board)
These are the products judges from Meta/HF will mentally compare your UI against. Your goal isn't to copy them — it's to match their information density and surpass their visual polish.
Vantage (https://www.vantage.sh) — The gold standard for clean FinOps UI. Their cost report view has a perfect balance of summary KPI cards at the top, a cost-over-time area chart in the middle, and a drilldown table at the bottom. Study how they handle the "resource list → detail panel" pattern. Their free tier screenshots are all over their docs.
CloudZero (https://www.cloudzero.com/dashboards/) — Focus on their "cost per customer" and "anomaly detection" views. They do the best job of connecting cost data to business context (which is exactly what your agent does — connecting waste to actions). Their dashboard gallery page shows multiple layout patterns.
Kubecost (https://www.kubecost.com) — Study their allocation view. It's the densest of the three — lots of bar charts breaking down cost by namespace/label/team. Good reference for how to show "cost breakdown by resource type" which your app needs. Now owned by IBM, so the design system is more enterprise-grade.
Infracost (https://www.infracost.io) — Interesting because they show cost before it's incurred (in PRs). Their UI for "here's what this change will cost" maps nicely to your "here's what this action will save" pattern.
Holori (https://holori.com/finops-dashboard/) — Good drag-and-drop dashboard builder reference. Study how they let users compose their own views from widgets.

Design Principles for Industrial B2B
A few things that separate "hackathon project" from "enterprise-ready" in the eyes of judges:
Information density over whitespace. B2B dashboards aren't marketing sites. Users want to see 15 metrics without scrolling. Study how Datadog and Grafana pack data into every pixel.
Neutral color palette with semantic accents. Gray/slate base. Green only for savings/success. Red only for waste/violations/alerts. Blue for informational. Never use color decoratively.
Data tables are first-class citizens. Your resource inventory table needs sorting, filtering, column resizing, row selection, and inline status badges. This is where B2B users live.
Progressive disclosure. Overview → click a wasteful resource → see detailed metrics → click "Apply recommendation" → confirmation modal. Never dump everything on one screen.
Dark mode is expected. Every FinOps tool ships with dark mode. It signals "this is a professional tool for engineers who stare at screens all day."
